Process Flow Overview

Sulfur Burning

Absorption
Dilution

1. Sulfur Burning (98%)

Reaction: S + O₂ → SO₂

Temperature: 900–1100°C | Pressure: ~1 atm

Highly exothermic reaction generating SO₂ stream.

Side reactions:

  • 2S + 3O₂ → 2SO₃
  • SO₃ + H₂O → H₂SO₄ (acid mist)

2. Gas Cleaning & Drying (98%)

Cooling, dust removal, and drying using concentrated H₂SO₄.

Temperature: 40–80°C | Pressure: Atmospheric

Prevents catalyst poisoning and corrosion.

3. Catalytic Converter (98%)

Reaction: 2SO₂ + O₂ ⇌ 2SO₃

Temperature: 420–450°C | Pressure: 1–2 atm

Catalyst: V₂O₅ (120 tons inventory)

Reversible exothermic reaction controlled for equilibrium.

4. Absorption Tower (98%)

Reaction: SO₃ + H₂SO₄ → H₂S₂O₇

Temperature: 60–80°C

SO₃ absorbed safely to form oleum.

5. Dilution (98%)

Reaction: H₂S₂O₇ + H₂O → 2H₂SO₄

Strongly exothermic – cooling required.
